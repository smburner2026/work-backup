## Nietzsche: Beyond Good and Evil

### Key Concepts Grid

| Concept | Sections | Core Claim |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Will to truth as problem | §1-4 | "Truth" worshipped for moral reasons, not epistemological ones. Falsity may be life-promoting. |
| Grammar critique / "I" as fiction | §17-19 | No stable subject behind thoughts. "It wills" not "I will." Will = commanding + obeying (social structure inside us). |
| Free will as weakness signal | §21 | Strong types don't debate free will. Weak types (socialists) weaponize determinism to dissolve responsibility. Nietzsche beyond both poles. |
| Kant critique | §11, §187 | Faculty-hunting as adolescent philosophy. German Idealism reached its end. |
| Will to power as ontology | §36 | Hypothesis that all force is will-to-power. Material world = more primitive form of emotional world. |
| Perspectivism | §12-16 | Science as interpretation, not "the real world." No view from nowhere. |
| Herd morality | §201-203 | Fear of distinction as core of modern morality. Democracy = Christianity's heir. |
| Death of God | §55 | Ultimate sacrifice—modern consequence of Christian truthfulness turning against itself. |
| Eternal recurrence | §56 | Introduced as "vicious circle." Connected to Zarathustra. |
| Saint as will to power | §51 | Turning drive inward. Recognized by the powerful as rival in same game. |

### User's Original Syntheses
1. Part One = clearing reader's prejudices, preparing for constructive philosophy (not mere demolition).
2. Nietzsche mischaracterized as anti-metaphysics—rejects *previous* metaphysics, not metaphysics itself.
3. Nature-critique §9 → psychology-critique §23 via genealogical method.
4. §§2-4 falsity/envy/justice = foreshadowing of revaluation.
5. §1 will-to-truth → §11 Kant-critique = architectural arc across Part One.
6. §36: material world = "more primitive form of world of emotions."

### Reading Method
- Section-by-section close. Constant cross-connection. Translation comparison (Kaufmann primary, Johnston secondary). Genealogical over analytical.

## Immunotoxicology (DABT)

### Core Framework: 4 Immunotoxic Outcomes
1. **Immunosuppression** — ↑ infection/cancer risk
2. **Immunostimulation** — cytokine release, autoimmunity risk
3. **Hypersensitivity** — Types I-IV (Gell & Coombs)
4. **Autoimmunity** — molecular mimicry, bystander activation

### Key Regulatory Path
STS (TG407/408) → signals? → ICH S8 WoE → TDAR/NK/host-resistance → risk assessment

### Hypersensitivity Types
- **I** IgE/mastcell → histamine → anaphylaxis
- **II** IgG/IgM cell-surface → complement/ADCC → cell destruction
- **III** Immune-complex deposition → tissue inflammation
- **IV** T-cell delayed → cytokine/macrophage → contact dermatitis (TG497)

### Immunosuppression: 4 Mechanisms
1. Direct kill (cyclophosphamide archetype)
2. Signal block (cytokine inhibitors)
3. Maturation interference (precursor block)
4. Stress-mediated (glucocorticoid → thymic apoptosis, reversible)

### Key Cases
- **Cyclophosphamide**: DNA alkylation → lymphocyte kill → TDAR ↓↓. Positive control for ICH S8.
- **TGN1412**: CD28 superagonist → cytokine storm. Illustrates intended≠safe.
- **Procainamide**: Drug-induced lupus. Molecular mimicry.
