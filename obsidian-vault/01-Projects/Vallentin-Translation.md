## About the Work

A George-Kreis portrait of Napoleon — not a military or political chronicle, but a **characterology of being**. Vallentin does what he says no one has done: show Napoleon's *figure* as a unity, drawing on the immediate sensory powers behind the man rather than cataloguing his qualities. Three opening sections lay out the method — then the body of the work moves through deed and experience (Book I), the experience of history (II), the classical type (III), feeling and mission (IV), God and faith (V), and art (VI).

The translation uses the register of Kantorowicz's *Frederick the Second* — high Edwardian-Georgian English with an archaic gravity befitting the subject matter. Dedication: "To the Hero of this Day" (Hodierno Heroi).

## Register Notes

The English register deliberately echoes Lorimer's Kantorowicz — a style that:
- Preserves the George-Kreis aphoristic weight
- Uses Latinate periods and hypotactic sentence architecture
- Favours "apparition" over "appearance", "being" over "character"
- Avoids flattening Vallentin's speculative density into academic clarity
