# Book: 16th Century France

> A Nietzschean portrait of the peak of European civilisation.

## Architecture

- **Opening:** Nietzsche's question — what made this civilisation great?
- **Closing:** Confrontation between Napoleon (action) and Nietzsche (thought)
- **Method:** Visionary portraiture after Burckhardt, not academic analysis
- **Register:** High, anti-academic, prose as weapon
- **Philosophical undercurrent:** The *moralistes* — Montaigne, La Boétie — physiology as lighting, not furniture

## Three Forces

| Force | Domain | Role |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Nomos** | Institutions, law, custom | Structure that channels civilisation |
| **Invisible Hand** | Economy, exchange, unintended order | The current beneath conscious action |
| **Great Individual** | The exceptional human | The spark that ignites eras |

Each chapter touches all three. Not separate treatments — integrated lens.
