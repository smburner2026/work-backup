# SchizoLiens — Entry Model Profile

**Type:** Manual / discretionary entry model
**Source:** Original SchizoLiens(TM) strategy document (PDF saved alongside)
**Style:** Trend-reversal / counter-trend (buy bottoms, sell tops, range-bound, chop)
**Weakness:** Poor performance in strong trending environments

---

## Core Structure

A 3-layer filter sequence for low-drawdown entries:

1. **HTF POI** — Higher Time Frame Point of Interest
2. **MSB** — Market Structure Break (1m chart)
3. **Fib Retracement Entry** — 0.5 / 0.618 / 0.75

---

## Layer 1 — HTF POI

- Identify areas of demand/supply on higher timeframe (support & resistance zones)
- These are the target zones where price is expected to reverse
- Wait for price to reach the POI before dropping to 1m chart

## Layer 2 — MSB (Market Structure Break)

- On the 1m chart, watch for a change in LTF market structure
- An MSB = a higher high formed with at least 1 full 1m candle body **entirely above** the previous high (not just a sweep)
- Indicates aggressive selling has subsided and momentum may flip
- Not straightforward to identify — requires attention throughout the POI

## Layer 3 — Fib Retracement Entry

After MSB confirmed, measure from the lowest low to highest high on LTF:

| Level | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| **0.618** | Standard retracement — preferred default |
| **0.5** | Aggressive trending move — frontrun the 0.618 |
| **0.75** | Deep chop — wide retracement expected |

The ideal move into the level is **swift** — price reacts immediately. Chances of continuation drop significantly if it lingers.

---

## Trade Management

- **Entry:** Limit order at the chosen fib level
- **Target 1:** First area of LTF supply → trim half, move stops to breakeven
- **Continuation:** Can run much further than the first target
- **Risk Management:** Hard stop at breakeven after first TP. The entry itself serves as invalidation if it fails.
- **Goal:** Zero-drawdown entry for an LTF trade that can be held for an HTF swing

---

## Limitations

- Picking the correct retracement level (context-dependent)
- No retracement after MSB → miss the entire move
- Contextual/confluential frontruns of fib levels (previous resistance wicks, structure)
- Crash straight through fib levels → probability + first 15m management

---

## Context & Confluence (Advanced)

- Multiple orders at key levels used in uncertain environments
- Previous resistance wicks near the 0.618 can cause a pico-level frontrun
- If retest isn't swift (spends minutes at level), expect a lower high → deeper retest
- Confluence factors can shift preferred entry level from standard fib values
