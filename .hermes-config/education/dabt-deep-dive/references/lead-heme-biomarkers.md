# Lead Heme Pathway & Biomarkers — Deep Dive Reference

## Exam Relevance

The 446-question database was searched for ALAD, ferrochelatase, heme, protoporphyrin, ZPP, basophilic stippling, and aminolevulinic. Only one question (DABT-0401) mentions the enzyme by name — as a distractor: "Overexpression of aminolevulinic acid dehydratase by lead" is the invalid pairing (lead INHIBITS ALAD, doesn't overexpress it).

No question in the database tests ferrochelatase by name. The exam tests the *pattern*, not the nomenclature.

## What's Tested

- Lead inhibits heme synthesis → microcytic hypochromic anemia with basophilic stippling
- Elevated urinary δ-ALA (earliest biochemical signal, ~10 µg/dL blood lead)
- Elevated zinc protoporphyrin / ZPP (~25-30 µg/dL blood lead)
- OSHA blood lead maintenance threshold: <40 µg/dL (removal at ≥50)
- CDC pediatric reference: 5 µg/dL

## Intuitive Framework: Production-Line Analogy

The heme pathway is a production line with two inspection gates lead can block:

**Gate 1 — ALAD (cytoplasm):** Takes δ-ALA and joins two molecules → porphobilinogen. Lead's –SH affinity inactivates this gate. When blocked, δ-ALA backs up and spills into urine. This happens at the lowest blood lead levels (~10 µg/dL) — the earliest biochemical signal.

**Gate 2 — Ferrochelatase (mitochondria):** At the end of the line, inserts Fe²⁺ into the porphyrin ring to make heme. Lead blocks it. Without iron, the empty ring (protoporphyrin) accumulates; zinc fills in, forming ZPP. Requires higher lead levels (~25-30 µg/dL).

## Biomarker Triad (Sufficient for Exam Pattern Recognition)

1. Elevated urinary δ-ALA → Gate 1 blocked (ALAD)
2. Elevated ZPP in erythrocytes → Gate 2 blocked (ferrochelatase)
3. Basophilic stippling on blood smear → disrupted erythropoiesis (ribosomal aggregates)

Clinical vignette: unexplained microcytic anemia + basophilic stippling + elevated urinary ALA + elevated ZPP = lead until proven otherwise.
